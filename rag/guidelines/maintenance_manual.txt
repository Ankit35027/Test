ENGINE TEMPERATURE GUIDELINES
If engine temperature exceeds 100°C, schedule immediate inspection within 24 hours.
Temperatures between 90-100°C indicate moderate risk; plan service within 7 days.
Normal operating temperature is below 90°C; routine check at next scheduled interval.

OIL QUALITY GUIDELINES
Oil quality score below 0.3 indicates critical degradation; replace oil immediately.
Scores between 0.3 and 0.6 suggest moderate wear; plan oil change within 500 hours.
Scores above 0.6 indicate acceptable oil condition; continue normal monitoring.

TIRE PRESSURE GUIDELINES
Tire pressure below 28 PSI requires immediate attention to avoid blowout risk.
Pressure between 28-32 PSI is acceptable but should be corrected at next service.
Optimal tire pressure is 32-36 PSI for standard fleet vehicles.

BATTERY VOLTAGE GUIDELINES
Battery voltage below 11.5V indicates failure risk; replace battery within 48 hours.
Voltage between 11.5V and 12.2V is weak; test and monitor closely.
Voltage above 12.4V indicates a healthy battery.

BRAKE CONDITION GUIDELINES
Poor brake condition requires immediate grounding of the vehicle until repaired.
Fair brake condition should be serviced within 2 weeks.
Good brake condition: inspect again at 10,000 km or 500 usage hours.

VIBRATION LEVEL GUIDELINES
Vibration level above 0.8 g may indicate suspension or drivetrain issues.
Levels between 0.4-0.8 g should be inspected during the next scheduled service.
Normal vibration is below 0.4 g.

HIGH RISK VEHICLE PROTOCOL
Any vehicle flagged as high maintenance risk should be reviewed by a fleet supervisor.
Recommended action: remove from active rotation, schedule full diagnostic within 48 hours.
Document all fault codes and maintenance history before servicing.

LOW RISK VEHICLE PROTOCOL
Vehicles with low maintenance risk should continue normal operation.
Schedule next predictive check at 500-hour intervals or monthly, whichever comes first.
Maintain usage logs for trend analysis.
